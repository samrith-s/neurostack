# Interleaving

Interleaving is the practice of mixing different topics or types of problems during study, rather than blocking (studying one topic exhaustively before moving to the next).

## Evidence

- Rohrer & Taylor (2007): Interleaved practice produced 43% better test performance than blocked practice
- Effective for both motor skills and cognitive tasks
- Benefits are greater for tasks requiring discrimination between categories

## Why It Works

Interleaving forces the brain to:
1. **Discriminate** between different concepts (which strategy applies here?)
2. **Retrieve** the relevant schema from long-term memory
3. **Compare and contrast** across categories

This generates productive [[prediction-errors-in-learning|prediction errors]] that strengthen category boundaries.

## Connection to Knowledge Vaults

A well-structured vault naturally supports interleaving:
- Cross-linked notes surface unexpected connections
- Community detection reveals thematic clusters
- Prediction error tracking flags notes appearing in unexpected contexts

The vault becomes an external interleaving engine — review sessions that traverse wiki-links across topics mirror interleaved practice.
